# Example ICP And Discovery Notes

Use this file when the product is sold to B2B teams that need better local or
SMB data, better enrichment, or cleaner TAM mapping.

This is not a universal B2B template.

It is one concrete buyer pattern from this repo.

## Example ICP

Best-fit companies:

- mid-market, early-growth B2B SaaS companies
- not SMBs and not large enterprises
- outbound team already exists
- team is scaling from roughly `5` to `20+` reps
- ACV above about `$5K`
- relies on good SMB contact data for prospecting

Typical buyers:

- Head of Revenue Operations
- Head of Sales Operations
- Head of GTM Engineering
- Salesforce Administrator
- Head of Sales can matter, but is usually not the primary buyer

Common downstream industries:

- general SMB software
- marketing agencies that sell to SMBs
- trades and home-services software
- medical or insurance software for small practices
- restaurant and hospitality software

This is the default ICP because the pain is operational, measurable, and tied
to outbound efficiency. It is a bad fit for junior-title outreach.

## Pain pattern

The strongest pain is usually not abstract data quality.

It is operational damage from bad data:

- reps calling wrong numbers
- lower connect rates
- fewer demos
- wasted sales time
- higher CAC
- weaker outbound ROI

The economic buyer usually feels the pain through efficiency and pipeline.

The end users are often:

- RevOps or SalesOps
- SDR teams
- whoever owns account sourcing and contact management

## Current status quo

Common current solutions:

- ZoomInfo
- Apollo
- manual scraping
- niche SMB-data providers

Common frustration:

- weak SMB coverage
- stale contact data
- too much manual cleanup
- too much work to map the full TAM

## Buyer and authority rules

Primary budget or purchase authority usually sits with:

- Head of Revenue Operations
- Head of Sales Operations
- sometimes an operations lead

Influencers often include:

- CRO
- VP Sales
- VP Revenue Operations
- SalesOps or RevOps team members

Common resistance:

- sales reps who do not trust new data
- leaders who are already embedded with the current vendor

## Discovery opener

Useful opener:

`Our process includes two parts: first, a discovery call to see if we're a mutual fit. Next step is a customized demo. Does that sound good?`

## Discovery questions

Use these to get to the real pain:

- How are you generating leads today?
- Walk me through the process.
- What is the toughest part of the process for you?
- How long has that been going on?
- How does it affect your goals?
- What goal are you trying to hit?
- What have you done to solve it?
- Why now?

Before a demo, also ask:

- Are there any other decision-makers involved?
- How quickly are you ready to improve this?
- Would you commit to working on this for a meaningful stretch if the fit is
  real?

## Qualification framework

Use this lens to decide whether the account is actually qualified:

- `Budget`: do they have money, tools, or internal resources already pointed at
  the problem
- `Authority`: who are the decision-makers and how does the buying process work
- `Needs`: what pain or use case is strong enough to justify change
- `Timing`: what time frame are they working against if this is a green light

If one of these is obviously weak, the account may still be interesting, but it
is not a clean qualified opportunity yet.

## Fit and no-fit rules

Better fit:

- bad data is clearly hurting prospecting
- TAM mapping is still messy
- they are open to proving quality with a sample or side-by-side test
- the buyer actually owns outbound efficiency or CRM/data quality

Weak fit:

- they want many custom features that are not realistic
- they do not have a hair-on-fire problem
- they are very happy with the current solution
- they are not trying to improve sales efficiency

## Selling motion notes

Best motion in this pattern:

- demo-first, not pure PLG
- prove quality before asking for a switch
- use a sample, side-by-side comparison, or real coverage test

The message should focus on the problem solved:

- cleaner enrichment
- better SMB coverage
- faster TAM mapping
- less manual cleanup

Do not lead with generic benefit language if you can name the actual workflow
friction instead.

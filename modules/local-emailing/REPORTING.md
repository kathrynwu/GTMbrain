# Weekly Reporting

Use this file when a campaign is live and you need to decide whether to keep,
pause, rewrite, or scale the segment.

The point of reporting is not to make the dashboard look clean.

The point is to learn:

- whether the segment is real
- whether the buyer is right
- whether the data is good enough
- whether the inbox setup is holding
- what buyers are actually saying back

## Review order

Review the campaign in this order:

1. open rate
2. response rate
3. meetings
4. pipeline
5. segment notes
6. customer notes

## Weekly thresholds

Use these repo defaults:

- open rate below `40%` -> check deliverability and data quality first
- response rate below `0.5%` with acceptable opens -> change segment and buyer,
  then rewrite the campaign
- response rate at or above `2%` -> keep or scale the segment
- no positive responses -> pause the segment

Do not confuse polite activity with a working segment.

Replies from the wrong buyer are often noise.

## What counts as a positive response

Count it as positive when the buyer does at least one of these:

- asks for a meeting
- asks for more information with real intent
- forwards internally to the right person
- confirms the pain is real and wants a follow-up

Do not count this as positive:

- polite interest with no authority
- curiosity from a junior title
- vague replies that do not move the process forward

## What counts as a dead segment

Pause the segment when:

- it produces no positive responses
- it keeps generating replies from the wrong buyer
- opens are acceptable but real buying intent never appears

Do not keep sending just because someone is replying.

## Diagnosis order when things are weak

When a campaign underperforms, check all four:

- list quality
- data quality
- segment quality
- deliverability

In practice, segment and data are often the first suspects.

Bad data in this repo usually means:

- bad phone
- bounced email

## Customer notes rule

Customer notes should record what you actually heard.

Do not reduce the call into your own biased summary.

Be specific. Exact language matters because patterns show up later.

Customer notes should capture:

- buyer title
- whether they have purchase authority
- exact objections
- current tool or vendor
- current lead-generation process
- what part of the process is hardest
- how long the problem has existed
- how it affects their goals
- what they have already tried
- why now
- other decision-makers involved
- timeline or urgency
- whether the account feels like a real fit

## Discovery and demo notes

For this repo's sales process, a useful discovery opener is:

`Our process includes two parts: first, a discovery call to see if we're a mutual fit. Next step is a customized demo. Does that sound good?`

Useful questions:

- How are you generating leads today?
- Walk me through the process.
- What is the toughest part of the process for you?
- How long has that been going on?
- How does it affect your goals?
- What is the goal you are trying to hit?
- What have you done to solve it?
- Why now?
- Are there any other decision-makers involved?
- How quickly are you ready to improve this?

## Common no-fit notes

Mark the account as weak or no-fit when:

- they want many custom features that are not realistic
- they do not have a real hair-on-fire problem
- they are happy with the current solution and not trying to improve
- they will not move off the current vendor

## Weekly output

A useful weekly review should end with:

- what segment to keep
- what segment to pause
- whether the buyer is wrong
- whether data quality is dragging results
- whether the campaign needs a full rewrite
- what exact customer notes changed the conclusion

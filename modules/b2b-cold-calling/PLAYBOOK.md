# B2B Cold Calling Playbook

Use this playbook when the list is clean enough to call and the B2B segment is
specific enough that a short phone conversation can create urgency.

If the motion is local-business calling, use [`../cold-call/`](../cold-call/).

The goal of the cold call is not to fully sell. The goal is to:

- verify the prospect is qualified
- show enough relevance to earn interest
- book the meeting

## Before you call

Know:

- company type
- buyer title
- pain hypothesis
- why your offer is better
- whether phone numbers are verified

Default buyer path in this repo:

- Head of Revenue Operations
- Head of Sales Operations
- Head of GTM Engineering
- Salesforce Administrator

Avoid junior-title calling as a default. It creates activity without authority.

Pressure-test the call target with:

- `Budget`
- `Authority`
- `Needs`
- `Timing`

If those are still weak, enrich the list before calling.

## The 3 steps

1. build credibility
2. build value
3. book the meeting

## 1. Building credibility

You have about 6 seconds to earn attention.

The opening needs 3 things:

1. conviction in your voice
2. straight-to-the-point delivery
3. a clear reason for the call

### Opener template

`Hi {name}, this is {rep} from {company}. Quick 30 seconds to explain why I'm calling?`

## 2. Building value

Building value means conveying 3 things:

- you understand their business
- you are credible in the category
- your product is better than the status quo

### Qualification prompts

- `How are you handling {problem} today?`
- `Is that working the way you want?`
- `What usually slows this down for your team?`
- `Who usually owns this on your side?`

## 3. Booking the meeting

The close should do 4 things:

1. define the next step
2. lock a day and time
3. send a calendar invite
4. get the invite accepted while still on the phone

### Close template

`The next step is a short walkthrough so we can show exactly how this would work for {company}. Are you free later today, or would tomorrow morning be better?`

## First-touch framework

Use these as framework shapes, not scripts to repeat word for word.

Rule:

- mention the problem as the hook
- then move into the value prop

Prep, `60s`:

- check news if relevant
- check LinkedIn
- check Crunchbase if relevant
- open the CRM page

Pattern:

1. opener:
   `Hi {name}, it's {rep} at {company}, can I steal 30 seconds to explain why I'm calling?`
2. hook:
   `I talk to RevOps leads drowning in bounced emails and stale contacts.`
3. value:
   `We push phone-verified contacts plus intent scores into Salesforce, cutting list-build time by 10 hours a week.`
4. discovery:
   `How are you handling contact accuracy today?`
5. close:
   `Worth a 15-minute compare? I'm free Tuesday at 10:00 or Wednesday at 3:00 PT.`

## Objection handling

Use the `A-R-P` model:

1. acknowledge
2. re-frame
3. permission

Common rebuttals:

- `Send me an email`
  `Happy to, so I send the best sample, which data headache tops your priority right now?`
- `Already have ZoomInfo`
  `Many of our clients keep ZoomInfo for >1k FTE firms and layer us for long-tail tech where coverage dips below what they need.`
- `No budget`
  `Understood. Most teams work with us out of time saved plus extra meetings within 60 days, open to a low-risk pilot?`
- `Not interested`
  `Fair enough. If I could prove 20% higher connect rates versus your current lists, would 10 minutes next quarter be valuable?`

## Follow-up and gatekeeper handling

Follow-up shape:

1. reference context
2. add new value
3. re-ask for the meeting

Example:

1. `Following up on the voicemail about your SDR list-build time.`
2. `Pulled a free 200-contact sample in your exact ICP.`
3. `Ten minutes tomorrow to review it?`

Gatekeeper route:

- ask confidently for the target by name
- if questioned, give brief value for the target's department
- politely ask for the best time or voicemail if blocked
- respect beats deception

## Metrics to watch

- connection rate
- qualified conversation rate
- meeting-booked rate
- show rate
- close rate

## Choosing the motion

- `A` call-first when the segment is tight, phones are good, and pain is urgent
- `B` enrich first when the list is broad or missing key data
- `C` email first when the segment needs more context before a call makes sense

## What good looks like

- strong first 6 seconds
- clear reason for the call
- real pain question before pitching
- value framed around the workflow problem
- buyer actually has authority
- concrete day and time locked

## What bad looks like

- vague intro
- polished conversation with the wrong buyer
- feature dump
- soft close with no meeting booked

## Sample talk track

These are reference examples. Do not repeat them word for word.

1. opener:
   `Hi {name}, it's {rep} with {company}, quick 30 seconds?`
2. hook:
   `The reason for my call is that most sales leaders tell me incomplete and dirty data is killing rep productivity.`
3. value:
   `We auto-enrich accounts and push phone-verified contacts into Salesforce in 60 seconds.`
4. discovery:
   `How much time did your team spend calling unqualified leads this week?`
5. proof:
   `Teams like DoorDash saved 10 analyst hours a week and hit 95% contact coverage in 30 days.`
6. CTA:
   `Worth 15 minutes tomorrow at 10 or 2 PT to see if we could replicate that?`
7. close:
   `Great, I'll send the calendar invite now. Appreciate the candor.`
